Pink Oregon Trail — Printing & Painting Guide

A beautiful hand-painted Oregon Trail board for Gabby’s history class. This guide walks through printing, painting, sealing, and using it for the game.

2. Staples printing instructions

  1. Walk into Staples or upload at staples.com.
  2. Choose Engineering Prints or Posters → 36×24" landscape.
  3. Paper: 100lb cover cardstock (best for paint) or satin photo paper (best for vivid colors as-is, no painting).
  4. Finish: matte for paintable, satin for the decorated print as-is.
  5. Cost: $15–30 depending on paper and rush options.
Note: True 36×24 watercolor paper is hard to find at Staples. Use acrylic paint thinned with water on cardstock for the best on-hand result.

3. Hobby Lobby supply list

  • Acrylic paint set (12-color basic)
  • Brushes: small detail (#2 round), medium fill (#6 flat), large wash (1" flat)
  • Palette or paper plate
  • Water cup & rag
  • Pencil & eraser (for sketching before painting)
  • Mod Podge gloss (for sealing afterward)
  • Optional: gold leaf paint pen (for the compass rose & cartouche)
  • Optional: fine-tip black marker (for re-tracing or detail work)

5. Painting techniques

Watercolor wash for sky

Wet the brush, load with light blue, paint the top edge of the region. Drag downward and lighten as you go. Pulling water across keeps a smooth gradient.

Layered green for grass and forest

Dark base layer first. Mid-tone over it once dry. Light highlights last. Three layers reads as depth without much detail.

Soft graduated mountains

Distant peaks in pale purple-gray. Foreground peaks in deeper brown-gray. Snow caps last, with a fine brush.

River highlights

Paint river blue. Once dry, add white streaks for current and reflections.

Painting around printed text

Don’t paint over the trail line, the space numbers, or the landmark labels. Paint up to them, leaving the printed details visible.

6. Sealing

  1. Wait 24 hours for paint to dry fully.
  2. Apply Mod Podge gloss with a foam brush, thin even coat, full coverage.
  3. Wait 24 hours.
  4. Optional second coat for extra durability.

Result: a board that survives a school year of handling.

7. Optional laminating

Staples laminating service ($5–10) for ultra-durability. Only use this on the FINISHED painted board, not on a paintable print.

8. Tips from Gabby’s dad

Sky first. It’s the easiest and gives you confidence.
Mountains in the background, not the foreground. Foreground = small details (people, trees, wagons).
Less paint is more paint. You can always add more. You can’t take it back.
If you mess up, the trail is forgiving. Real pioneers had bad days too.
