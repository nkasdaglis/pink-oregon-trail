# 🌾 Pink Oregon Trail — Board Assembly & Shopping Guide

For Gabby and her mom

---

## 📐 What you're making

A folding game board, 25.5 inches wide × 17 inches tall. That's about the size of a Settlers of Catan or Ticket to Ride board — perfect for a classroom table. The board is made from **6 pieces of letter-size paper (8.5 × 11 inches)** arranged 3 across and 2 down, glued onto a stiff backing.

Two versions are included:
- **Decorated** — the trail, all 25 stops, mountains, buffalo, salmon, pine trees, state names — already drawn for you
- **Paint Your Own** — just the trail outline and stop circles, ready for you to paint and decorate however you like

You can mix and match (decorated top row, paint-your-own bottom row, for example).

## 🛠️ Step-by-step assembly

**Total time: about 2-3 hours, plus drying time overnight.**

### Step 1 — Print the tiles (15 minutes)

You have 12 HTML files in your project folder (6 decorated + 6 blank). Pick which version of each tile you want:

- All decorated → easiest, prettiest result
- All blank → most fun for painting
- Mix → decorated top row, blank bottom row, or whatever you choose

For each tile you want:

1. Open the HTML file in any web browser (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox).
2. Press **Ctrl+P** (Windows) or **Cmd+P** (Mac) to print.
3. Choose **Letter** size, **Portrait** orientation, **margins: minimum or 0.3 inches**.
4. Print on **good quality paper** if you can — 24-pound paper or cardstock prints crisper than regular printer paper. White is fine; cream looks more period-appropriate but is not necessary.

Print all 6 tiles you've chosen. **Don't trim them yet.**

### Step 2 — Lay it out (10 minutes)

1. On the floor or a big table, arrange the 6 sheets in this pattern:

```
┌──────────┬──────────┬──────────┐
│  Tile 1  │  Tile 2  │  Tile 3  │   ← TOP ROW
│   (A)    │   (B)    │   (C)    │     (Independence → Foothills)
├──────────┼──────────┼──────────┤
│  Tile 6  │  Tile 5  │  Tile 4  │   ← BOTTOM ROW
│   (D)    │   (E)    │   (F)    │     (Oregon City ← Mountains)
└──────────┴──────────┴──────────┘
```

The trail flows: **Top row left to right (Tiles A → B → C)**, then **drops down to the bottom row right side (Tile F)**, then **bottom row right to left (F → E → D)**, ending at Oregon City on the bottom-left.

2. Check the alignment marks at the edges of each tile — they tell you which tile connects to which.

### Step 3 — Trim the edges (15 minutes — Mom's job, with the craft knife)

1. Each tile has dashed lines marking where to trim. Trim just to those lines.
2. Trim along the edges that will JOIN to other tiles. Don't trim outside edges.
3. Tiles will now fit together edge-to-edge with no white margin between them.

### Step 4 — Cut the foam board (5 minutes — Mom's job)

1. Lay the 6 trimmed tiles in their final pattern on the foam board.
2. Mark the outline with a pencil — should be about 25.5 × 17 inches.
3. Cut the foam board to that size with the craft knife. Use the cutting mat underneath to protect the table.

### Step 5 — Glue down the tiles (30 minutes + drying time)

1. Pour a puddle of Mod Podge on the foam board.
2. Use the foam brush to spread it in a thin even layer over a section of the board (one tile area at a time).
3. Place the first tile (Tile A) on the upper-left corner. Smooth it from the center outward to push out air bubbles.
4. Repeat for the other 5 tiles. Make sure adjacent tiles butt up cleanly with no gap and no overlap.
5. Let dry **at least 1 hour** (longer is better).

### Step 6 — Paint! (only if you chose blank tiles — variable time)

This is where Gabby gets creative. Some painting suggestions:

- **Trail dirt color:** brown acrylic with a little black mixed in. Paint over the dashed guide line.
- **Plains:** pale yellow-green wash for grass.
- **Mountains:** purple-gray for the peaks, white for snow caps.
- **Rivers:** blue with a little white swirled in for movement.
- **Forts:** brown with tiny black "log" lines.
- **Start (Independence):** bright green with gold highlights.
- **Finish (Oregon City):** gold leaf paint or metallic gold!

Add details like:
- Tiny wagon shapes near the start
- A buffalo silhouette on the plains
- Pine trees in the forest area
- A salmon in the Columbia River

Let everything dry **at least 2 hours**.

### Step 7 — Seal the board (15 minutes + overnight drying)

1. Apply a thin coat of Mod Podge OVER the entire surface of the board (paper and paint together).
2. Use the foam brush. Light strokes, all in one direction.
3. Let it dry **overnight**.

This seals everything, makes the board waterproof and durable, and gives it a nice finish.

### Step 8 — Make the wagon tokens (15 minutes)

1. If you bought tiny plastic wagons: paint them or decorate them. Each team picks one.
2. If you bought wooden discs: paint each one a different color (white, pink, blue, green) — one per team in Competitive mode. Add a tiny number or letter to identify whose is whose.

## 🎲 How to use the board with the game

1. **Open the game** by double-clicking `pink_oregon_trail.html`.
2. **Place the board** on the classroom table where everyone can see it.
3. **Place the wagon tokens** on Space 1 (Independence, MO).
4. **One person drives the keyboard** — they make the choices, click the buttons.
5. **Everyone else watches the screen AND the board.** When the screen says "Move your wagon to Space 7," the person whose turn it is moves their physical token on the board.
6. **The screen tells the story.** The board shows progress.

The board is also brilliant after the game ends:
- Leave the wagons where they ended up (whether they finished or were stopped by something).
- Hang the board on the classroom wall as a permanent record of the journey.
- Different class periods can play and leave their own marks of where they got.

## 💡 Bonus ideas if you have time

- **Make team tokens** — instead of just one wagon per team, make 7 tiny tokens to represent each team member. As members are lost in the game, remove their tokens from the board. Visceral and emotional.
- **Make weather cards** — small index cards with sun / rain / snow that someone can place on the current space when storms happen.
- **Make a "graves" pile** — tiny tombstone shapes from cardstock. Place one beside the trail at every space where someone died. Over a class period of multiple games, the trail gets ghostly.
- **Make a journal box** — a small box on the side of the board where the printed Trail Journals go after each game. End of semester, the class has a stack of stories.

## 🆘 Help!

- **Tiles don't line up?** Check that you trimmed to the dashed lines (not outside them).
- **Mod Podge made the paper bubble?** Lay a heavy book on top while it dries.
- **Spilled paint on a finished section?** Let it dry, then paint over with the cream background color. It'll cover.
- **Foam board is bigger than 25.5 × 17?** Just trim it down with the craft knife. Don't worry about being exact.
